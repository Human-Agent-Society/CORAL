# Research Quality Checklist

Use this checklist to self-review your research before moving to implementation.

## Completeness

- [ ] Problem objective and evaluation criteria are clearly stated
- [ ] At least 3 web searches were conducted on the broad topic
- [ ] At least 2 specific techniques were investigated
- [ ] Domain-specific concepts relevant to the task are explained
- [ ] Existing attempts and shared notes were checked

## Evidence Quality

- [ ] Claims are backed by sources (papers, benchmarks, documentation)
- [ ] URLs are included for key references
- [ ] Performance numbers are cited where available
- [ ] Limitations of cited methods are acknowledged

## Approach Selection

- [ ] At least 2 candidate approaches are documented
- [ ] Trade-offs between approaches are explicit
- [ ] Selected approach has clear justification
- [ ] Implementation feasibility is considered (available libraries, complexity)

## Actionability

- [ ] Research summary is written and saved to shared notes
- [ ] Implementation plan has concrete steps
- [ ] Key libraries/tools needed are identified
- [ ] Potential failure modes and fallbacks are noted

## Common Issues to Check

| Issue | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| No sources cited | Add URLs from your searches |
| Only one approach considered | Search for alternatives |
| Approach chosen "because it's familiar" | Evaluate on evidence, not comfort |
| Research doesn't connect to evaluation metric | Re-read grader code, tie findings to scoring |
| Duplicates existing research in notes | Reference the existing note, add only new findings |
